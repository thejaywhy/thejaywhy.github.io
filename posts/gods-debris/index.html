Why are we here? 'God's Debris'


I re-read Scott Adams’ God’s Debris: A Thought Experiment after 10 years. This book was a pretty profound read for me when I was in college - it really helped me define my relationship with religion. In college I thought it was the most amazing thing ever - but I was oh so young.

Yes, this book is written by that Scott Adams, creator of Dilbert. This book has nothing to do with Dilbert. In the introduction, Adams challenges the reader to discern what is truth, and what is made to look like truth.

The book centers on two characters, and a discussion of “Why?”. It really is a thought experiment that asks why things are, the way the are - or are they? Confused yet?

To me, the biggest revelation I got from this book is that a God may have existed at some time, and that we are its’ debris from some explosive event. We are governed by the Law of Probabilty - and that our level of consciousness is related to our ability to understand that our minds are not perfect. We see the world through various belief systems - none of which are the “true” way.

Religion and Science are both described as belief systems that can be used to describe and define the world around us.
